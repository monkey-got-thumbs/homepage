Why "just use AI" isn't the whole answer

AI is powerful but still unreliable — today's models can confidently get things wrong. Lean on it blindly and those mistakes slip through. So the skill isn't using AI more; it's understanding it well enough to know when to trust it and when to check.

Good news: that's a learnable skill, not a technical one. You don't need to code or understand the maths — you need a few good habits.

The Smarter Path: Augmented Intelligence

Augmented intelligence is the practice of pairing human judgment with machine capability. You stay in control. AI handles the mechanical work — research, drafting, data processing. You handle the thinking — strategy, judgment, relationships.

Harvard research shows that professionals using AI this way produce 40% higher quality work and complete tasks 25% faster. The key? They knew how to direct it.
