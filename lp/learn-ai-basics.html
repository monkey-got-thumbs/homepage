Understand AI in 10 Minutes. No Jargon, No Hype.

AI is everywhere. Most explanations are either too technical or too breathless. Here's what you actually need to know.

What AI Actually Does

AI — specifically large language models like ChatGPT and Claude — predicts the next most likely word in a sequence. It's a very good pattern-matcher rather than a thinker — a difference worth keeping in mind as you weigh what it gives you. Under the hood it's an extraordinarily sophisticated system trained on vast amounts of text.

This makes it genuinely useful for all sorts of work: drafting text, summarising documents, generating code, processing data. It also has a few honest limitations worth knowing — and they're easy to allow for once you can spot them.

It can get things wrong — confidently

AI sometimes states things that aren't true, and it says them just as smoothly as the right answers. A quick check from you keeps it honest.

It can't really explain itself

When AI "explains" its reasoning, that's more generated text — not a window into actual logic. It reads more like a helpful draft than the final word.

It's still early days

The tools are improving fast and the ground keeps shifting. There's an upside there — no settled expertise to be behind on yet, so now is an easy time to get comfortable.

It works best with you

Used well, AI can lift the quality of your work — and how much it helps tends to track what you bring: your direction and judgement.

A Better Approach: Augmented Intelligence

Instead of treating AI as a replacement for human thinking, augmented intelligence treats it as an amplifier. You direct. AI assists. You verify. AI generates. You decide. AI processes.

The case for it: reclaimed time, higher-quality output, and skills that may grow more valuable every year rather than less.
