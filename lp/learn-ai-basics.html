Understand AI in 10 Minutes. No Jargon, No Hype.

AI is everywhere. Most explanations are either too technical or too breathless. Here's what you actually need to know.

What AI Actually Does

AI — specifically large language models like ChatGPT and Claude — predicts the next most likely word in a sequence. It's a very good pattern-matcher rather than a thinker — which is exactly why your judgement matters. Under the hood it's an extraordinarily sophisticated system trained on vast amounts of text.

This makes it genuinely useful for all sorts of work: drafting text, summarising documents, generating code, processing data. It also has a few honest limitations worth knowing — once you do, you get far more out of it.

It can get things wrong — confidently

AI sometimes states things that aren't true, and it says them just as smoothly as the right answers. A quick check from you keeps it honest.

It can't really explain itself

When AI "explains" its reasoning, that's more generated text — not a window into actual logic. Treat it as a helpful draft, not the final word.

It's still early days

The tools are improving fast and the ground keeps shifting. That's good news — getting comfortable now puts you ahead of the curve.

It works best with you

Used well, AI lifts the quality of your work — and you're the key variable. Your direction and judgement are what make it shine.

The Better Approach: Augmented Intelligence

Instead of treating AI as a replacement for human thinking, augmented intelligence treats it as an amplifier. You direct. AI assists. You verify. AI generates. You decide. AI processes.

The result is a compounding advantage: reclaimed time, higher quality output, and skills that get more valuable every year — not less.
