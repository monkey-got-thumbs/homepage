Understand AI in 10 Minutes. No Jargon, No Hype.

AI is everywhere. Most explanations are either too technical or too breathless. Here's what you actually need to know.

What AI Actually Does

AI — specifically large language models like ChatGPT and Claude — predicts the next most likely word in a sequence. That is it. It does not think, reason, or understand. It is an extraordinarily sophisticated pattern matcher trained on vast amounts of text.

This makes it genuinely useful for certain tasks: drafting text, summarising documents, generating code, processing data. But it also means it has fundamental limitations that no amount of training will fix.

It makes things up

LLMs hallucinate 3-27% of the time. They present fiction as fact with complete confidence.

It can't explain itself

AI cannot trace its reasoning. The "explanations" it gives are themselves generated text, not actual logic.

It's losing money

OpenAI projects $14B in losses for 2026. The business model is not proven.

It needs you

AI directed by a skilled human produces 40% better results. The human is the key variable.

The Better Approach: Augmented Intelligence

Instead of treating AI as a replacement for human thinking, augmented intelligence treats it as an amplifier. You direct. AI assists. You verify. AI generates. You decide. AI processes.

The result is a compounding advantage: reclaimed time, higher quality output, and skills that get more valuable every year — not less.
