Just the facts (from the video)

  • Debt is the running total of every yearly deficit (plus interest), minus surpluses. The US ran a deficit in 92 of the last 125 years.
  • FY2024: took in $4.9T, spent $6.8T → a $1.8T deficit. Treasury borrows via bills, notes & bonds (you can buy at treasurydirect.gov).
  • 2024 interest: $880B — the 3rd-largest federal expenditure, above Medicare and defense; $269B of it went abroad.
  • Make-up (2024): $28.86T held by the public + $7.36T intragovernmental (what the government owes itself, e.g. Social Security).
  • Of public debt: ~70% American, ~30% foreign. Top foreign holders: Japan > China > UK — together 8.8% (≈$2.5T).
  • Debt-to-GDP (public): 95.9% in 2024, up from 72.4% a decade earlier — up in all but 15 of the last 50 years.
